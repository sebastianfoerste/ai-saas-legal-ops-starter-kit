# Template: Board Legal & Regulatory Risk Memo

## 1. Purpose
This template provides a concise executive briefing document summarizing regulatory risks, material customer commitments, and compliance posture for the Board of Directors.

## 2. When to Use
Use this template to prepare quarterly board briefs, executive committee updates, or when alerting leadership to critical, un-remediated risk exposures.

## 3. Intake Questions (To be completed by General Counsel / Risk Owner)
*   **Executive Sponsor**: [Name / Title, e.g. Sebastian Förste, General Counsel]
*   **Reporting Period**: [e.g. Q2 2026]
*   **Target Audience**: [Board of Directors / Executive Leadership Team]
*   **Date of Briefing**: [YYYY-MM-DD]

## 4. Review Questions (To be completed by Risk Owner)
*   **Material Risk Matters**: [Summarize top regulatory exposures, e.g. DORA compliance gaps, EU AI Act preparation]
*   **AI Vendor Governance Posture**: [Number of AI tools reviewed, approved, rejected, and open issues]
*   **Customer Commitment Exposure**: [Any active or at-risk custom commitments that could trigger SLA penalties]
*   **Regulatory Horizon Scanning**: [Brief summary of incoming rules, e.g. local data sovereignty laws, new AI frameworks]

## 5. Output
*   **Executive Risk Summary**: [High-level paragraph for executive reading]
*   **Fulfillment Tracking Action**: [Mitigation steps, timelines, and budgets needed]
*   **Required Action**: [e.g. Board approval of AI Vendor Policy v2 / Budget allocation for compliance audit]

## 6. Escalation Triggers
This memo must be immediately routed to the CEO and Board Chair if:
*   A material compliance gap is identified that could result in fines exceeding 1% of annual revenue.
*   An active customer lawsuit or data breach occurs.
*   A critical subprocessor loses SOC 2 compliance status without an immediate backup migration plan.

## 7. Playbook Update
*   *Incorporate feedback from the board into the annual risk register, and update risk scoring thresholds in the automated triage software.*
